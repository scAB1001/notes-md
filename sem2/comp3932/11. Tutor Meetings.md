### Meeting takeaways
**Good Sources**
- Aim for 10--20 references for the Literature Review
- When searching, use the Advanced Search feature: NOT Title Contains "Review"
- Opt for peer reviewed sources
	- When citing a non-peer reviewed source mention so to show critical thinking: e.g. this source is not peer-reviewed so cannot be taken at face-value
- [Springer Nature](https://www.springernature.com/gp#)
- [Taylor & Francis](https://www.tandfonline.com/)
- [Science Direct](https://www.sciencedirect.com/)
- [British Medical Journal](https://www.bmj.com/)

**Review Articles**
- Review Articles are useful for finding primary sources. Review Articles are not primary sources.
- Don't cite the review article itself, cite who they have reviewed
- If you wish to use something in a review article like a useful point/takeaway, mention that what your taking comes from the review article which looked at the original source.

**Approach to Background Research and the Literature Review**
- Its all about combining stuff together
- Save/bookmark/download any paper that seems interesting and relevant in some way
- Aim to skim-read one per day: 
	- Write a paragraph
	- Get Citation
