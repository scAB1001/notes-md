### Neat
Academic projects should provide evidence of a much  
deeper understanding of what you are doing. They require  
justification and insights. You are not expected to merely do  
what you are told to do; you are expected to develop and  
explain thoughts, arguments, ideas, and concepts. You are  
expected to question things and look at things in new ways  
and from new angles.  

Merely doing what you are told does not lead to intellectual  
discovery or contributions to knowledge. As a degree  
student you are expected to think, and to demonstrate your  
thinking in a written report. This ‘deeper’ understanding of  
situations, problems, and events is supported by your  
research skills. Demonstrating your ability to undertake an  
academic project is a critical component of your degree  
course

Dawson outlines five features of computing projects. These are not  
mutually exclusive; your project should include three or more categories.  
The nature of your project will influence the methods you use to tackle it.  
- Review-based: systematic review of solutions to related tasks  
- Development: software / system development  
- Evaluation: comparing alternative solutions to a task  
- User-based: solving a requirement for a “client/user”  
- Problem Solving: a new technique to solve a known problem
